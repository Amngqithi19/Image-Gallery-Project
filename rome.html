ROME

the capital city of Italy and one of the most monumental cities in World History and an integral part of the world today. Rome has and always will be a city of class and stature, an important figure in many aspects of the world, it is home to the Vatican City, the cornerstone of the Catholic Church and governs a huge part of the Christian Religion today and also holds a very significant part of the Italian Renaissance. A long long time period ago, Rome was home to many emperors, such as the Ceasar's and one of those being Julius Ceasar and Rome is home to the Colossuem, the Pantheon and many more, it is a tourist destination one should really take into account for one period of their lifetime.





Places you can visit in the capital of Italy





Colossuem

the colosseum, the cornerstone of an age where brawls, fighting was part of the Roman culture and where citizens would come and watch people compete against man or even beast. although today it is a much more peaceful environment, the colosseum is a great destination if somebody wants to entangle themselves in the Italian culture and enjoy themselves





Pantheon

the pantheon is a great depiction of the great skill and calibre the Roman people had even back in the olden days where we nowadays, we would call it a primative existence. the pantheon proves to the world the excellence that the Roman Empire once possessed





Roman Forum

the Roman Forum is the cornerstone of Renaissance Italy's Marketplace and is a beautiful destination to visit due to its environmental setting and the majestic columns that are still standing up til this day from the days of the Roman Empire





Sistine Chapel

the Sistine Chapel is a catholic church that is most famously known for hosting the event of the papl conclave, the process of appointing a new pope for the Catholic church and its famous artistic designs, the Sistine Chapel ceiling and the painting, The Last Judgement which were done the great artist Michelangelo





St. Peters Basilica

the St. Peters Basilica is a catholic church in the Vatican City and is known as the largest church by interior measure. Much like the Sistine Chapel, it is an important part of the catholic church's tradition and one of the greatest achievements of renaissance Italy
